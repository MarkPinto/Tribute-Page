Gabe Newell

The man who revolutionized PC gaming as well as the gaming landscape

A timeline of important events in Gabe Newell's personal and work life:

  • 1962 - Born in Seattle, Washington

  • 1980 - Attends Harvard University

  • 1983 - Drops out of Harvard University to work with Microsoft as a computer programmer

  • 1996 - Gabe quits Microsoft and starts his own company Valve Corporation and also marries Lisa Mennet the same day

  • 1998 - The game Half-Life is released which at the time was a game that had the most advanced AI and graphics

  • 2003 - The Steam client is released, which will become the biggest distribution platform for games on PC

  • 2009 - The first major sale on the Steam store, the holiday sale happens, which would later be a staple of the store with future Summer and Winter seasons

  • 2012 - Valve introduces Steam greenlight, a way for the community to decide whats games should go on the market, a new entry for indie developers to get into the market

  • 2013 - Gabe receives British Academy of Film and Television Arts fellowship award for his contributions to games

  • 2013 - Steam OS is released as a way for Valve to take away marketshare from Microsoft Windows

  • 2015 - Valve introduces paid mods, Gabe and the company are met with huge backlash from the community and get rid of paid mods shortly after

  • 2015 - Valve tries to attract the console crowd with the intorduction of Steam machines, Steam controller and the Steam Link

  • 2015 - Valve allows refunds of games bought through their store

  • 2016 - Valve partners with HTC to debut the HTC Vive, a virtual reality headset Valve's entry into the VR gaming market

  • 2017 - Valve shuts down Steam greenlight and replaces it with Steam Direct, allowing anyone with $100 to publish their game onto the market

  • 2018 - Proton is introduced to the Steam client, allowing Windows only games to run better on Linux
